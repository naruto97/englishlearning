# ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY CHEAT SHEET
## Quick Reference for Distinguished (100/100)

---

## POINT BREAKDOWN

| Section | Points | Key Requirements |
|---------|--------|------------------|
| Introduction | 15 | 4+ sentences, engaging hook, clear thesis with 3 reasons |
| Body Paragraphs | 15 | 3 paragraphs, 4+ sentences each, 1 claim each, detailed examples |
| Organization | 15 | Logical flow, strong explicit transitions |
| In-Text Citations | 15 | Every claim cited, proper MLA format |
| Conclusion | 15 | 4+ sentences, ALL points summarized, no new info |
| Works Cited | 15 | Zero formatting errors, MLA 9 format |
| Mechanics | 10 | Zero errors, varied sentences, formal tone |

---

## INTRODUCTION FORMULA

```
[HOOK: 1-2 sentences - statistic, question, scenario, or bold statement]
[CONTEXT: 1-2 sentences - explain debate, name alternatives]
[THESIS: Although [counterargument], [position] because [reason 1], [reason 2], and [reason 3].]
```

**Hook Types:**
- Statistic: "3.7 million seniors face..."
- Question: "What if the choice at eighteen..."
- Scenario: "Every May, seniors stand at a crossroads..."
- Bold: "This is the most consequential decision..."

**Avoid:** "Many people...", "This essay will...", "In today's society..."

---

## BODY PARAGRAPH FORMULA (TEEL)

```
T - Topic sentence (ONE claim, connects to thesis)
E - Evidence (specific data WITH citation)
E - Explanation (why evidence matters)
L - Link (to thesis or next paragraph)
```

**Transition Words:**
- Addition: "Beyond...", "In addition to...", "Equally important..."
- Contrast: "While...", "Although...", "Despite..."
- Conclusion: "The evidence demonstrates...", "These findings confirm..."

**Avoid:** "Also," "Another point is," "In addition," (too weak)

---

## MLA CITATION QUICK GUIDE

**Parenthetical:**
```
"Claim with data here (Author)."
```

**Signal Phrase:**
```
"According to [Source], claim with data here."
```

**Rules:**
- Citation goes BEFORE the period
- Use (Author) or (Organization)
- Every statistic needs a citation

**Avoid:** "Studies show..." (vague), citation after period, redundant citations

---

## CONCLUSION FORMULA

```
[RESTATED THESIS: Same meaning, different words]
[SUMMARY: Touch on ALL 3 body paragraph topics]
[REFLECTION: Deeper insight, acknowledge nuance]
[FINAL THOUGHT: Memorable closing]
```

**Must include:**
- Reference to Body ¶1 topic
- Reference to Body ¶2 topic
- Reference to Body ¶3 topic

**Avoid:** New information, "In conclusion," copying intro thesis

---

## WORKS CITED FORMAT

```
                              Works Cited

Author Last, First. "Article Title." Website Name, Publisher, Date,
        URL. Accessed Date.
```

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Separate page
- [ ] "Works Cited" centered (NOT bold)
- [ ] Alphabetical order
- [ ] Hanging indent
- [ ] Double-spaced
- [ ] Access date included

## MECHANICS QUICK FIXES

| DON'T | DO |
|-------|-----|
| don't, can't, won't | do not, cannot, will not |
| I think, I believe | Evidence suggests, Research indicates |
| you, your | students, graduates, one |
| a lot, really | significantly, substantially |
| things, stuff | factors, elements, considerations |

**Sentence Variety:**
- Simple: "College graduates earn more."
- Compound: "College is expensive, but returns are substantial."
- Complex: "Although costly, college provides long-term value."

## COMMON POINT LOSSES

| Mistake | Point Loss |
|---------|------------|
| Only 3 intro sentences | -6 |
| Only 2 body paragraphs | -3 |
| Missing citations | -3+ |
| Missing Works Cited | -15 |
| Not mentioning all points in conclusion | -3 to -6 |
| Comma splices | -1 to -4 |

- [ ] Every claim has MLA citation
- [ ] Transitions between ALL paragraphs
- [ ] Conclusion mentions ALL 3 body topics
- [ ] Works Cited is formatted correctly
