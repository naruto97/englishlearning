### Exercise 10: Paragraph Checklist

Review a body paragraph you've written and check each element:

☐ Topic sentence states ONE claim
☐ Topic sentence connects to thesis
☐ At least one piece of specific evidence
☐ Evidence is properly cited (MLA)
☐ Evidence is explained (not just stated)
☐ Explanation answers "why does this matter?"
☐ Paragraph ends with link to thesis or transition
☐ Paragraph has 4+ sentences
☐ No first person (I, me, my)
☐ No second person (you, your)

## ANSWER KEY

### Exercise 1 Answers:

**Topic Sentence A:**
- Problem: Multiple claims (four topics crammed in)
- Fixed: "College graduates enjoy substantially higher earnings throughout their careers than those who skip higher education."

**Topic Sentence B:**
- Problem: Leads with evidence, not claim
- Fixed: "College graduates earn significantly more than their non-degreed peers, a gap that widens over time."

**Topic Sentence C:**
- Problem: Vague, doesn't state specific claim
- Fixed: "The financial returns of a college education far exceed those of alternative paths."

---

### Exercise 3 Sample Transitions:

**¶1 → ¶2:**
"Beyond these compelling financial advantages, college also provides career flexibility that becomes increasingly valuable in a changing economy."

**¶2 → ¶3:**
"While these advantages are substantial, advocates for trade schools and immediate employment raise objections that deserve consideration."

---

### Exercise 4 Sample Specific Claims:

**General → Specific:**
"According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, workers with bachelor's degrees earn a median weekly salary of $1,432, compared to $853 for those with only high school diplomas (Bureau of Labor Statistics)."

---

### Exercise 7 Sample Explanations:

**For $579/week gap:**
"This $579 weekly difference may seem modest in isolation, but it compounds dramatically over time. Annually, this gap represents over $30,000 in additional income. Over a forty-year career, college graduates accumulate more than $1.2 million in additional earnings—enough to fund retirement, purchase property, and achieve lasting financial security."
