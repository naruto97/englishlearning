## ANSWER KEY

### Exercise 1 Fixes:

**A Problem:** Citation after period
**Fixed:** "College graduates earn more money (Bureau of Labor Statistics)."

**B Problem:** Source named twice (redundant)
**Fixed:** "According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, graduates earn more."

**C Problem:** Period inside parentheses
**Fixed:** "Research shows significant earnings differences (Bureau of Labor Statistics)."

**D Problem:** Citation completely outside sentence
**Fixed:** "The earnings gap exceeds one million dollars (Carnevale)."

### Exercise 2 Answers:
- Set A: **a** (citation before period)
- Set B: **b** (signal phrase doesn't need parenthetical)
- Set C: **c** (proper quote integration)

### Exercise 3 Answers:
- A: NEEDS CITATION (specific dollar amount)
- B: NO CITATION NEEDED (your analysis)
- C: NEEDS CITATION (specific percentage)
- D: NO CITATION NEEDED (common knowledge)
- E: NEEDS CITATION (specific percentage)
- F: NO CITATION NEEDED (your conclusion)

### Exercise 4 Sample Corrected Paragraph:

> "College graduates earn significantly more than high school graduates. Weekly earnings for degree holders average $1,432, compared to $853 for those with only high school diplomas (Bureau of Labor Statistics). Over a lifetime, this gap compounds to approximately $1.2 million in additional earnings (Carnevale). By age 34, college graduates typically surpass trade workers in annual income, and this gap continues widening (Federal Reserve Bank of New York). These financial realities demonstrate that college is a sound investment."

### Exercise 10 Problems:

1. "Studies show" is vague—no specific citation
2. "over a million dollars" needs citation
3. "(research)" is not a valid source name
4. Missing period after quote before closing quotation mark
5. "Trade workers earn less" needs citation
