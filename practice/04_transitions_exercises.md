## ANSWER KEY

### Exercise 1 Answers:
- A: Addition (adding new benefit)
- B: Contrast (introducing opposition)
- C: Conclusion (summarizing)
- D: Addition
- E: Contrast

### Exercise 2 Sample Strong Transitions:

**A:** "Beyond these compelling financial advantages, college also provides career flexibility that becomes increasingly valuable in a changing economy."

**B:** "Equally important to long-term success is the job security that a college degree provides."

**C:** "While these benefits are substantial, advocates for trade schools raise objections worth examining."

**D:** "When weighing immediate costs against long-term outcomes, the evidence consistently favors higher education."

### Exercise 4 Answer:
**Best order: C, B, A**
- C (Earnings) first: Most concrete, data-driven, establishes credibility
- B (Flexibility) second: Expands beyond money, builds on financial argument
- A (Counterargument) third: Addresses objections after making your case

### Exercise 5 Answers:
- Arrangement A: Strategy 1 (Strongest First)
- Arrangement B: Strategy 3 (Problem-Solution-Defense)
- Arrangement C: Strategy 2 (Strongest Last)

### Exercise 6 Problems:

**Essay A:** Random order—strongest argument (earnings) is buried; social life doesn't connect to other points

**Essay B:** Counterargument first—leads with opposition's points, puts you on defense

**Essay C:** Thesis in wrong place—should be in introduction, not conclusion

### Exercise 7 Ratings:

**Pair A:** 2/5 — "Too" is weak; no connection to previous point
**Improve:** "Beyond these financial returns, college provides career flexibility that proves equally valuable over time."

**Pair B:** 5/5 — Smooth transition that connects ideas
**No improvement needed**

**Pair C:** 1/5 — Abrupt, no transition, sounds like counterargument but isn't framed as such
**Improve:** "While these advantages are compelling, proponents of trade school argue that their faster path to employment offers benefits worth considering."
