# Section 6: Works Cited Page - Comprehensive Examples

## RUBRIC REQUIREMENTS (Exact Language)

**Distinguished (15 Points):**
> Works Cited page follows formatting and MLA citation guidelines.

**Proficient (12 Points):**
> Works Cited page has two or three errors in formatting but follows MLA citation guidelines.

**Basic (9 Points):**
> Works Cited page has four to five errors in MLA formatting and citation guidelines.

**Below Expectations (6 Points):**
> Works Cited page has more than five errors in MLA formatting and citation guidelines.

**Non-Performance (0 Points):**
> Works Cited page was not submitted.

**CRITICAL NOTE:** This is the only category where Non-Performance = 0 points (not 3). The Works Cited page is absolutely required.

---

## POINT BREAKDOWN: What Separates Each Level

| Points | Formatting | Citation Format | Error Count |
|--------|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 15 | Correct | Correct MLA | 0-1 errors |
| 12 | 2-3 errors | Correct MLA | 2-3 errors |
| 9 | 4-5 errors | 4-5 errors | 4-5 errors |
| 6 | 5+ errors | 5+ errors | 5+ errors |
| 0 | Not submitted | Not submitted | N/A |

**Key Insight:** Even small formatting errors count. To earn 15 points, your Works Cited must be essentially perfect.

---

## FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS

### Requirement 1: Separate Page
- Works Cited begins on its OWN page
- Insert a page break after conclusion
- Should be final page of document

### Requirement 2: Title
- "Works Cited" centered at top
- NOT bold
- NOT underlined
- NOT italicized
- Just regular text, centered

### Requirement 3: Spacing
- Double-space EVERYTHING
- Between title and first entry
- Between entries
- Within entries (if entry spans multiple lines)

### Requirement 4: Alphabetical Order
- Entries listed A-Z
- By first word of entry (usually author's last name)
- If no author, by organization name or title

### Requirement 5: Hanging Indent
- First line: flush left (at margin)
- Subsequent lines: indented 0.5 inches
- Creates "hanging" effect

---

## MLA 9 WEB SOURCE FORMAT

### Complete Format (All Elements)

```
Author Last, First Name. "Article Title." Website Name, Publisher,
        Day Month Year, URL. Accessed Day Month Year.
```

### Element-by-Element Breakdown

| Element | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Author | Last, First. | Carnevale, Anthony P. |
| Article Title | "In quotes." | "The College Payoff." |
| Website Name | *Italicized*, | *Georgetown University Center*, |
| Publisher | (if different from site), | Georgetown University, |
| Date | Day Month Year, | 5 Aug. 2021, |
| URL | url. | cew.georgetown.edu/reports/. |
| Access Date | Accessed Day Month Year. | Accessed 28 Dec. 2025. |

## WHAT TO DO: Distinguished-Level Works Cited Entries

### Entry Type 1: Website with Individual Author

**Correct Format:**
```
Carnevale, Anthony P. "The College Payoff." Georgetown University Center
        on Education and the Workforce, Georgetown University, 5 Aug.
        2021, cew.georgetown.edu/cew-reports/the-college-payoff/.
        Accessed 28 Dec. 2025.
```

**Element Analysis:**
- Author: Last name, First name. ✓
- Article title: In quotes, period inside ✓
- Website: Italicized, comma after ✓
- Publisher: Listed because different from website name ✓
- Date: Day Month Year format ✓
- URL: Included, ends with period ✓
- Access date: Included ✓
- Hanging indent: Second+ lines indented ✓

---

### Entry Type 2: Organization as Author

**Correct Format:**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
        /careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
        Accessed 28 Dec. 2025.
```

**Element Analysis:**
- Organization as author: No period after, just starts ✓
- Article title: In quotes ✓
- Website name: Italicized ✓
- Publisher: Government department listed ✓
- Date: Month Year (no day available) ✓
- URL: Complete path ✓
- Access date: Included ✓

---

### Entry Type 3: Article with No Author

**Correct Format:**
```
"Why College Still Matters." The Chronicle of Higher Education, 15 Mar.
        2024, www.chronicle.com/article/why-college-still-matters.
        Accessed 28 Dec. 2025.
```

**Element Analysis:**
- No author: Start with article title ✓
- Article title: In quotes ✓
- Website: Italicized ✓
- Date: Full date included ✓
- No separate publisher (website IS the publisher) ✓
- URL: Included ✓
- Access date: Included ✓

---

### Entry Type 4: Government Report

**Correct Format:**
```
Federal Reserve Bank of New York. "The Labor Market for Recent College
        Graduates." Federal Reserve Bank of New York, 15 Oct. 2024,
        www.newyorkfed.org/research/college-labor-market. Accessed
        28 Dec. 2025.
```

**Element Analysis:**
- Organization as author ✓
- Report title in quotes ✓
- Website same as author (repeated) ✓
- Full date ✓
- URL ✓
- Access date ✓

## WHAT NOT TO DO: Works Cited Errors

### Error Category 1: Title Formatting

**Error A: Bold Title**
```
                                    **Works Cited**
```
*Why wrong:* MLA requires plain text, not bold

**Error B: Underlined Title**
```
                                    Works Cited
                                    ___________
```
*Why wrong:* MLA requires plain text, not underlined

**Error C: Quotation Marks Around Title**
```
                                    "Works Cited"
```
*Why wrong:* Title should be plain text, no quotes

**Error D: Wrong Title**
```
                                    Bibliography
```
*Why wrong:* Must be titled "Works Cited" for MLA

**Correct:**
```
                                    Works Cited
```

---

### Error Category 2: Spacing Issues

**Error A: Single-Spaced**
```
Works Cited
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics...
Carnevale, Anthony P. "The College Payoff." Georgetown University Center...
```
*Why wrong:* All entries must be double-spaced

**Error B: Extra Space Between Entries**
```
Works Cited

Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023."...


Carnevale, Anthony P. "The College Payoff."...
```
*Why wrong:* Consistent double-spacing only, no extra gaps

**Correct:**
```
Works Cited

Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023."...

Carnevale, Anthony P. "The College Payoff."...
```

---

### Error Category 3: Alphabetization Issues

**Error: Wrong Order**
```
Works Cited

Federal Reserve Bank of New York. "The Labor Market..."

Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays..."

Carnevale, Anthony P. "The College Payoff..."
```
*Why wrong:* F comes after B and C; should be B → C → F

**Correct Order:**
```
Bureau... (B)
Carnevale... (C)
Federal... (F)
```

---

### Error Category 4: Hanging Indent Problems

**Error A: No Hanging Indent**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
/careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
```
*Why wrong:* Second and third lines should be indented

**Error B: First Line Indented (Reverse Indent)**
```
        Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau
of Labor Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023,
www.bls.gov/careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
```
*Why wrong:* First line should be flush left; subsequent lines indented

**Correct (Hanging Indent):**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
        /careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
```

---

### Error Category 5: Citation Format Errors

**Error A: Missing Period After Author**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics "Education Pays, 2023."
```
*Why wrong:* Period required after author name

**Error B: Article Title Not in Quotes**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. Education Pays, 2023.
```
*Why wrong:* Article titles must be in quotation marks

**Error C: Website Name Not Italicized**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor...
```
*Why wrong:* Website name should be italicized

**Error D: Period Outside Quotation Marks**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023".
```
*Why wrong:* Period goes INSIDE closing quotation mark

**Error E: Missing Access Date**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
        /careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
```
*Why wrong:* Web sources need "Accessed Day Month Year."

---

### Error Category 6: Date Format Errors

**Error A: Wrong Month Abbreviation**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." ... September 2023...
```
*Why wrong:* Months longer than 4 letters are abbreviated (Sept., not September)

**Error B: Wrong Date Order**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." ... 2023, September...
```
*Why wrong:* Order should be Day Month Year (5 Sept. 2023)

**Correct Month Abbreviations:**
- Jan., Feb., Mar., Apr., May, June, July, Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.
- Note: May, June, July are NOT abbreviated

---

### Error Category 7: URL Issues

**Error A: Hyperlink Formatting Left In**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." ... https://www.bls.gov...
```
If the URL appears as a clickable blue link, that's a formatting issue.

**Error B: "Retrieved from" Before URL**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." ... Retrieved from
        www.bls.gov...
```
*Why wrong:* MLA doesn't use "Retrieved from" (that's APA style)

**Correct:**
```
...www.bls.gov/careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
```

---

### Error Category 8: Missing or Mismatched Entries

**Error A: Citation in Essay, Not on Works Cited**
If you cite "(Carnevale)" in your essay but have no Carnevale entry on Works Cited, you lose points.

**Error B: Entry on Works Cited, Never Cited in Essay**
If you have an entry for a source you never actually cite, remove it.

**Rule:** Every in-text citation = matching Works Cited entry
Every Works Cited entry = at least one in-text citation

## COMPARISON: CORRECT VS. INCORRECT

### Example Entry: Bureau of Labor Statistics

**INCORRECT (Multiple Errors):**
```
"Education Pays, 2023". Bureau of Labor Statistics. www.bls.gov. 2023
```

**Errors:**
1. Article title comes first (should be author/org first)
2. Period outside quotation marks
3. Website not italicized
4. URL incomplete
5. No "Accessed" date
6. Missing publisher
7. Date format wrong

**CORRECT:**
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
        /careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm.
        Accessed 28 Dec. 2025.
```

## STEP-BY-STEP: Building an Entry

### For a Web Article with Organization Author

**Source Information:**
- Organization: Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Article: "Education Pays, 2023"
- Website: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics
- Publisher: U.S. Department of Labor
- Date: September 2023
- URL: www.bls.gov/careeroutlook/2023/data-on-display/education-pays.htm
- Accessed: December 28, 2025

```
Bureau of Labor Statistics.
```

```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023."
```

```
        Statistics,

```
```
Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Education Pays, 2023." U.S. Bureau of Labor
```
```
        Statistics, U.S. Department of Labor, Sept. 2023, www.bls.gov
```

## SELF-CHECK QUESTIONS

1. **Separate Page:** Is my Works Cited on its own page after the conclusion?

2. **Title Check:** Is "Works Cited" centered and in plain text (not bold, underlined, or italicized)?

3. **Spacing Check:** Is everything double-spaced?

4. **Alphabet Check:** Are my entries in A-Z order by first word?

5. **Indent Check:** Does each entry have hanging indent (first line left, rest indented)?

6. **Match Check:** Does every in-text citation have a Works Cited entry?

7. **Reverse Match:** Is every Works Cited entry actually cited in my essay?

8. **Format Check:** Does each entry have: Author. "Title." *Website*, Publisher, Date, URL. Accessed Date.

9. **Punctuation Check:** Are periods inside quotation marks? Commas between elements?

10. **Date Check:** Are months abbreviated correctly?

## WORKS CITED CHECKLIST

Before submitting, verify:

**Page Formatting:**
- [ ] Works Cited on separate page (after conclusion)
- [ ] "Works Cited" centered, not bold/underlined/italicized
- [ ] Entire page double-spaced
- [ ] Entries in alphabetical order (A-Z by first word)
- [ ] Hanging indent on all entries

**Entry Formatting:**
- [ ] Each entry has author or organization first
- [ ] Article titles in quotation marks
- [ ] Website names italicized
- [ ] Periods inside closing quotation marks
- [ ] Commas between elements (website, publisher, date)
- [ ] Months abbreviated correctly (Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.)
- [ ] URLs included (no "Retrieved from")
- [ ] Access dates included ("Accessed Day Month Year.")

**Matching:**
- [ ] Every in-text citation has matching Works Cited entry
- [ ] No Works Cited entries for sources not cited in essay
